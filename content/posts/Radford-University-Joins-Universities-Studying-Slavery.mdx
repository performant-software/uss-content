Please Welcome Radford University to the Universities Studying Slavery (USS) Consortium!

Universities Studying Slavery is proud to welcome Radford University in Radford, Virginia, as its newest member institution. Radford joins a growing international consortium of more than one hundred colleges and universities committed to research, acknowledgment, and atonement regarding institutional ties to slavery, racism, and their enduring legacies.

Radford's path to USS membership was shaped by its participation in the USS Inclusive History Certificate Program, a joint initiative of Universities Studying Slavery and the Jesse Ball du Pont Foundation. In 2025, Radford completed the Certificate Program as a participant in the "Preparing to Launch" pathway — a track designed for institutions building the foundational infrastructure needed to undertake rigorous inclusive history work. That experience directly informed the shape and ambitions of Radford's flagship research initiative.

That initiative, the Radford University Community History Research Collective (RUCHRC), convenes in 2025–2026 and is housed within the Office of the Provost. The RUCHRC brings together research experts from across campus alongside community partners, connecting them with students, alumni, neighbors, and other interested constituents to discover, preserve, and communicate the stories of the institution's past and its deep connections to Southwest Virginia.

The Collective's work is oriented toward a significant milestone: the 125th anniversary of Radford's 1910 founding as "a State normal and industrial school for white women," to be observed in 2035. Anchored in Radford University's commitment to nurturing a sense of place in Appalachian Virginia, the RUCHRC will illuminate an inclusive institutional history — from the decades before that founding to the present day — in order to foster deeper ties between the university and its neighboring communities.

The RUCHRC's project co-director professor Geoffrey Pollick captured the spirit of the moment: "I want to thank USS for your support, guidance, and inclusion over the last eighteen months as Radford has begun to explore doing the work of studying our institution's ties to enslavement and what that might look like in our particular context. Our project team is so excited to take the next steps and truly get underway with learning how to do this work and build connections with our community."

By joining USS, Radford gains access to a rich network of peer institutions sharing best practices, research strategies, and hard-won experience in the challenging and vital work of honest institutional self-examination. We are delighted to welcome Radford University to the USS family.
