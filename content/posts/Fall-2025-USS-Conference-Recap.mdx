We were thrilled to see so many of you in Houston October 10-13 for the Second Foundings Conference ([https://taskforce.rice.edu/second-foundings](https://taskforce.rice.edu/second-foundings)) at Rice University. The four days of events highlighted the USS collaborative spirit, connecting USS membership from afar with the Texas Consortium of Universities and Colleges Studying Slavery and Race. Conference panels and events were held primarily at Rice University, with a full day hosted at Texas Southern University. 

Watch a great video recap here: [https://news.rice.edu/news/2025/second-foundings-conference-brings-national-dialogue-slaverys-legacies-rice](https://news.rice.edu/news/2025/second-foundings-conference-brings-national-dialogue-slaverys-legacies-rice) 

We wanted to highlight some of the most powerful moments from the conference: 

• Ruth J. Simmons’ moving call to action in her keynote address 

• Rice University students and alumni delivering plenary poetry readings and performances of their musical compositions reflecting on Rice University’s history 

• Touring the amazing Texas Southern University (TSU) student Hannah Hall murals documenting sixty years of life at the HBCU 

• A screening of The Route to Emancipation and Encountering the Ancestors documentaries 

• Two days of research poster sessions highlighting work by Prairie View A & M University, Texas Southern University, University of Virginia, and Fort Bend ISD students 

• Crystal Sanders’ engaging keynote address at TSU 

And that’s just the greatest hits from a conference packed with learning. Please thank Rice University and the conference program committee for their fine work!
